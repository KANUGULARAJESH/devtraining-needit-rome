## Speaker Notes (2–3 Minutes)

> "This slide compares the two orchestration approaches for executing dbt transformations on Databricks. Both approaches are technically feasible, but after evaluating scalability, operational overhead, and maintainability, we recommend Option 1."

> "The process begins with the dbt project stored in Git. Every code change goes through the CI/CD pipeline, where the Databricks Asset Bundle packages the dbt project and deploys the Databricks Job consistently across Development, Staging, and Production environments."

### Option 1 – Recommended

> "In the recommended approach, Airflow is used purely for orchestration. It schedules the workflow and triggers a pre-deployed Databricks Job using the DatabricksRunNowOperator."

> "The Databricks Job starts a dedicated Job Cluster, where dbt Core executes commands such as `dbt deps` and `dbt build`. During execution, dbt compiles the models, resolves dependencies, and generates SQL. Using the dbt-databricks adapter, the compiled SQL is submitted to the Databricks SQL Warehouse, which executes the queries and materializes the Silver and Gold models in Unity Catalog."

> "This architecture clearly separates orchestration from execution, minimizes Airflow maintenance, provides consistent deployments through Databricks Asset Bundles, and allows Databricks to manage compute scaling and dependencies."

### Option 2 – Alternative

> "In the second approach, Airflow executes dbt directly using BashOperator or DbtTaskGroup. Here, dbt Core and the dbt-databricks adapter must be installed and maintained on the Airflow workers. The Airflow worker compiles the dbt models and submits the SQL to the Databricks SQL Warehouse for execution."

> "While this approach works, it increases operational overhead because Airflow must manage dbt versions, adapters, dependencies, and authentication. It is also more difficult to scale and maintain consistently across environments."

### Recommendation

> "For the Holcim implementation, we recommend Option 1 because it follows Databricks best practices. Airflow focuses only on orchestration, while Databricks handles dbt execution and compute management. This provides better scalability, simpler operations, consistent deployments, and easier long-term maintenance for enterprise workloads."

This explanation fits comfortably within **2–3 minutes** and covers the architecture, execution flow, comparison, and recommendation at the right level for a technical audience.
